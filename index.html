Puppet Community

Modules and tooling for and by the Puppet Community.

Follow me onGitHub

This organisation is trying to synchronise certain community efforts. The idea is to have a namespace, maintained by the community with no official relation to Puppet Labs, where we can develop our modules and tooling. We have a google group here.

Rationale

The reason we want to do this is because every now and then what Puppet Labs wants for the future of a module or a piece of software clashes with what the community wants. This gives us a place to experiment on our own and go with what we as a community feel we need. We might be wrong, but we can try.

Modules

Another benefit we hope to achieve is that people will eventually start moving their modules to this community, especially when they feel they no longer have or want to maintain a module. People change jobs, tools or simply outgrow their current solutions but orphaned modules are a pain to those that still rely on them. Storing them with the Puppet Community will allow others to more easily step in and take over ensuring continued development.

Tooling

Over the years a lot of tooling on top of Puppet has been developed. Some of those, though in active use, have fallen into a state of decay because the maintainer is AWOL or simply doesn't have the time or need to maintain this tool. The consequence of this is that a forest of forks with different patches and features have arisen and no one knows which one to pick, nor has an guarantee that the fork they're now using will see further development.
